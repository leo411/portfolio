junior front end developer @ eZ-nergy

This is my first full-time job as a junior front end developer. My role was to add new features and functionalities to a SAAS application written in Angular. I occasionally worked on the back end of the project, written in C#. Working with a large team of highly skilled developers has allowed me to rapidly expand my coding competencies and level of autonomy . My boss in particular has been a wonderful mentor to me in helping me expand my coding knowledge. We work using the Agile methodology.

The opportunity to build features starting from an API route on the backend and taking it all the way to Angular templates on the frontend has been both a pleasure and a rewarding experience. Working on a mature code base has really accelerated my learning curve, and I’ve been amazed at how fast my competencies and autonomy have evolved, particularly with Angular.

We would do a scrum on a daily basis and after my boss had presented the tasks of the sprint every 2 weeks I would go and work (mostly) autonomous on each of my task. I make sure I have follow every leads I read the documentation and would do an online tutorial if need be before I ask for assistance. I strongly believe that I remember better something that I would have figure it out on my own. Of course I don't hesitate to ask for assistance when I feel I won't come up with a solution myself.

Working with Angular and C# on a daily basis, I have a stronger understanding of OOP (Object Oriented Programing). We used GitLab as a version control system and YouTrack by JetBrains as an agile board to support the scrum.

With the Covid-19 epidemic I’ve been working completely remotely with the team. In spite of only working at a distance, I’ve been excited to find just how much I can contribute to the code base and advance in my own learnings even from afar, both in a coding sense and also in understanding the complex core business. Though credit must also go to the whole tech team who has been incredibly welcoming and supportive.
